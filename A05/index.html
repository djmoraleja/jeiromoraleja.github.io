Parish and National Shrine of St. Padre Pio

Pambansang Dambana at Parokya ni Santo Padre Pio

"Pray, hope, and don't worry."

— Saint Padre Pio

About

The Parish and National Shrine of Saint Padre Pio is a Roman Catholic parish church and pilgrimage site in Santo Tomas, Batangas. It is consecrated to Saint Pio of Pietrelcina.

Designated a national shrine by the CBCP—the first in Batangas—it also hosted the 4th World Apostolic Congress of Mercy in 2017.

Architecture

The shrine features indigenous materials such as wood, bamboo, stone, and nipa. The roof is shaped like a Filipino salakot, topped by an image of Our Lady of Mercy. This open and eco-friendly design suits the tropical climate perfectly.

Inside, a replica of the Glorious Cross hangs above, and the altar, crucifix, and lectern are decorated with driftwood. The Blessed Sacrament Chapel and Baptistery are situated behind and below the main altar.

History

From a small chapel on Maharlika Highway, the parish was canonically erected in 2003. A larger church was built on donated land in 2006. The shrine was dedicated in 2013 and declared a national shrine in 2015.

Devotion to Saint Padre Pio

Every 23rd of the month, prayer and healing services are held at 9 AM and 5 PM. Pilgrims venerate relics and receive the anointing of the sick. Red and white roses symbolize joy through suffering and divine grace. Religious souvenirs are available at the shrine store.

Location

The Parish and National Shrine of St. Padre Pio is located at Barangay San Pedro, Santo Tomas, Batangas, Philippines.
